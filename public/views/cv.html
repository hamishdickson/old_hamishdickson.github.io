More about me

What interests me

I've recently been using the MEAN architecture (MongoDB, Express.js, Angular.js, Node.js) a lot and it's great. Single page webapps have become very powerful over the years (I remember when Google Maps first came out and all I could say was "WOW! How did they do that?!"), MEAN is great for that - you can make something really cool in just a few hours.

I'm really interested in breaking how big, hard problems. I love trying to think about turbulence and how the problem could be broken out into smaller chunks that could be modelled. This is normally what I'm thinking about when I'm on the tube or in the shower or going for a walk.

What I'm good at

I'm at my best when I've been given a problem which no body really understands and told to find the solution. This is also the bit of work I'm best at.

I'm good at teaching. Over the last few years I have taught lots of new graduates to code and analyse problems.

No one can work on their own all the time, I've always been good at working with people (I'm a fan of a type of pair programming) and I'm very good at getting my ideas across to people.

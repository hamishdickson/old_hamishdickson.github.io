To London or not to London?

So in the interests of full disclosure - I grew up all over the world. My father worked for a giant oil company when I was young and so it was normal for me to move every year or so.

Having said that, I've now lived in London for 13 years. I came to university here, enjoyed every second so went out my way to stay.

I now work for a company who makes software for investment banks. While not the most rewarding work, I work with great people and love working with them.

So here is my problem: London is insane.

Let me break that down a bit, firstly it's impossible for a normal person to buy a house here. I earn an OK wage (I could earn more doing similar work at a bank, but as explained above it's not that rewarding and hey, I like the people I work with) but have no real savings (certainly not that 50 grand plus needed for a deposit).

Secondly: The Tube. If that needs explaining, then you've probably not been to London.

Thirdly: It's expensive.

So these points (and many more) would be worth it if I went out a lot or did lots of things in London, but that really doesn't happen - I get up, code some stuff, go to work, go to the gym, come home and crash out. Then do it all again. I don't take advantage of this place.

Hamish - 17th May 2015
